Introduction 01 / 06

Why This Guide Exists

If you're reading this, you're probably carrying something. A decision that keeps surfacing. A conversation you've been avoiding. A choice that feels too big to get wrong.

After 20+ years leading a family business—navigating succession, letting people go, making P&L decisions that affected real lives—I learned something important:

The decisions we avoid have a compounding cost. And the clarity we seek is usually closer than we think.

This guide gives you the 5 questions I use with every leader I work with. They're designed to cut through the noise and help you see what's really at stake—and what's really holding you back.

How to Use This Guide

  • Set aside 20-30 minutes of uninterrupted time
  • Think of the one decision that's weighing on you most
  • Answer each question honestly—write in the spaces provided
  • Notice where you hesitate or feel resistance

Signs You're Ready to Decide

  • You've named the decision specifically
  • You understand what you're afraid of
  • You can articulate the cost of waiting
  • You know what you'd tell someone else
  • You can picture what peace looks like

Self-Assessment 05 / 06

Warning Signs You're Avoiding a Decision

Sometimes we don't realize we're avoiding something. Here are the patterns I see most often in leaders who are stuck:

Endless Research

Gathering more data when you already have enough to decide.

Waiting for Perfect

"I'll know when the time is right" — but it never feels right.

Seeking Consensus

Needing everyone to agree before you'll move forward.

Delegation Disguise

Handing off decisions that are ultimately yours to make.

Busy-ness Shield

Filling time with urgent tasks to avoid important ones.

Rationalization Loop

Finding reasons why "now isn't the right time."
